IPL History · 2008 – 2025
2008 · Season 1
Who won the inaugural IPL and who was the breakout star?
Rajasthan Royals won, captained by Shane Warne. Yusuf Pathan and Sohail Tanvir were the stars. Brendon McCullum opened the tournament with an iconic 158* for KKR.
2009 · Season 2
IPL 2009 was played outside India — why, and who won?
Security concerns due to general elections moved the tournament to South Africa. Deccan Chargers, captained by Adam Gilchrist, beat RCB in a low-scoring final.
2010 · Season 3
Which team lifted their first IPL trophy in 2010?
Chennai Super Kings won their maiden title, beating Mumbai Indians. Suresh Raina and MS Dhoni were the pillars. CSK became the first team to defend a final spot back-to-back.
2011 · Season 4
CSK back-to-back — what made the 2011 campaign special?
CSK retained their title, making them the first team to win consecutive IPL championships. Murali Vijay was the standout batter, and the final was played at Chennai's MA Chidambaram Stadium.
2012 · Season 5
KKR ended a long wait — who led them to glory?
Kolkata Knight Riders won their first title under Gautam Gambhir's calm captaincy. Sunil Narine's bowling mystery was virtually unplayable that season, conceding under 5.5 per over.
2013 · Season 6
Mumbai Indians' first title — who was the hero of the final?
MI beat CSK in a final at Eden Gardens. Rohit Sharma led by example and Kieron Pollard played a decisive cameo. This was also the season when spot-fixing controversies hit the league hard.
2014 · Season 7
KKR's second title — what was remarkable about this campaign?
KKR retained the trophy, making Gautam Gambhir the first captain to win IPL twice. Manish Pandey made history by becoming the first Indian to score an IPL century (114* vs RCB).
2015 · Season 8
Mumbai won again — who was their most consistent performer?
Mumbai Indians beat CSK in the final, with Lendl Simmons playing a match-winning knock. Lasith Malinga's death-over bowling was the cornerstone of their bowling attack all season.
2016 · Season 9
Who were Sunrisers Hyderabad's key players in their first title?
SRH, captained by David Warner, beat RCB in the final. Ben Cutting hit 39 off 15 balls to seal it. It was RCB's best season yet — Kohli scored 973 runs, but the final slipped away.
2017 · Season 10
IPL's 10th season — who was the Player of the Tournament?
Mumbai Indians won their third title, edging Rising Pune Supergiant by just one run in the final. Ben Stokes played a brilliant 68 in a losing cause. Rohit Sharma's calm captaincy was decisive.
2018 · Season 11
CSK returned from a two-year suspension — what happened?
Chennai Super Kings came back from a suspension ban and immediately won the title, beating SRH. Shane Watson's 117* in the final was one of the greatest IPL innings ever played.
2019 · Season 12
The most dramatic final finish — what happened on the last ball?
Mumbai Indians needed 8 off 3 balls, then 2 off 1 ball vs CSK. Lasith Malinga bowled Shardul Thakur to win MI's fourth title by 1 run — arguably the greatest IPL final ever played.
2020 · Season 13
IPL moved to UAE during COVID — who dominated?
Mumbai Indians won a record 5th title, beating Delhi Capitals. Ishan Kishan and Quinton de Kock gave MI explosive starts throughout. Trent Boult's new-ball spells were decisive in the knockouts.
2021 · Season 14
CSK bounced back after a poor 2020 — who was key?
CSK won their fourth title, beating KKR in a one-sided final. Faf du Plessis was the highest run-scorer. It was widely seen as MS Dhoni's masterclass in rebuilding a team mid-tournament.
2022 · Season 15
Two new teams joined — who surprised everyone?
Gujarat Titans, in their very first IPL season, won the title under Hardik Pandya's captaincy, beating Rajasthan Royals. Shubman Gill and Mohammed Shami were the standout performers.
2023 · Season 16
CSK's 5th title — who was the hero of the final?
Chennai Super Kings beat Gujarat Titans in a rain-affected final. Devon Conway played a crucial 47* to anchor the chase. It was MS Dhoni's 5th IPL title as captain — the most ever.
2024 · Season 17
KKR dominated the season — what made their campaign special?
Kolkata Knight Riders won their third title, beating SRH convincingly in the final. Mitchell Starc, bought at a record ₹24.75 crore, delivered. Venkatesh Iyer's all-round form was the season's revelation.
2025 · Season 18
Which team finally ended their long IPL title drought in 2025?
Royal Challengers Bengaluru won their first-ever IPL title after 18 seasons of heartbreak, beating Punjab Kings in the final. Virat Kohli, in his landmark IPL year, finally lifted the trophy at Chinnaswamy.
Fantasy Cricket Strategy
Batting Captain Pick Bowling Transfers
Differential captain: A non-obvious captain separates your team when the favourite underperforms.
Budget wicket-keeper: One budget WK frees credits to stack two premium batters or an elite bowler.
Lock after toss: Always wait for the toss before finalising your XI — pitch and playing conditions change everything.
Multi-team approach: Create 2–3 variants with different captains and VCs to maximise rank ceiling.
Chase form over reputation: A batter in top form over the last 5 games beats a star who hasn't fired in 3 matches.
Venue history matters: Check a bowler's economy at the specific ground — some venues are death traps for pace.
Home advantage: Home teams win ~55% of IPL games — weight your picks from the home XI, especially openers.
All-rounder stacking: Pick all-rounders from both teams — they score across all categories and give you maximum point surface area.
Avoid injured returnees: A player back from injury in their first game rarely fires — give them one match before selecting.
Powerplay openers first: T20 fantasy rewards powerplay aggression — an opener who goes big in overs 1–6 delivers the most bang per credit.
Read the chatter: Pre-match press conferences and social media often reveal player fitness and team combination — check them 2 hours before deadlines.
Night match edge: Dew after over 15 in evening games makes it harder to grip the ball — spinners picked to bowl in the middle overs are the safer bet.
